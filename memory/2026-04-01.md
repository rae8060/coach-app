**Key Literary Discoveries:**
- **Simile technique**: "blankets like a tent" - makes you visualize
- **Sound/rhythm**: "BANG" - short words create tension
- **Dialogue humor**: Ron's phone call ("I WANT TO TALK TO HARRY POTTER!") - capitalization shows volume
- **Sentence rhythm mirrors action**: Aunt Marge's inflation - sentence length expands with the inflation
- **Specific details build world**: "Bathilda Bagshot" - tiny names create depth

**Chapter-by-Chapter Analysis (Prisoner of Azkaban):**
- Chapters 1-2: Setup, birthday presents, Aunt Marge explosion
- Chapter 3: Knight Bus introduction - Stan Shunpike's dialect
- Chapters 4-7: Dementors, Boggart lesson, Buckbeak, Sirius Black setup
- Chapters 8-10: The tension builds, Marauder's Map introduced

**Literary Techniques We Noted:**
- Foreshadowing (the Grim, Scabbers/Peter)
- Dramatic irony (we know the truth via Map, Harry doesn't)
- Character foils (Lupin vs Snape)
- Planting & payoff (Sneakoscope, Crookshanks, Time-Turner)
- Themes: fear vs courage, monster within, second chances
